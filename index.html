Dr. Lise Meitner

Biography

Lise Meitner(born November 7,1878,Vienna,Austria-Hungary _ died Octobber 27,1968,Cambridge,England) was an austrian physicist who contributed to the dicovery of nuclear fission and was amongst the first to isolate the isotope protactinium-231 after a three decades of work along with Hahn. She is the first women in Austria to earn a doctorate in physics .

Key moments in Lise Meitner's life:

                    
    
                            
  • 1878 – Born in Vienna, Austria.
    • 
                            
  • 1905 – Earned a doctorate in physics, becoming one of the first women in Austria to do so.
                            
    • 
                            
  • 1907 – Moved to Berlin and began working with Otto Hahn on radioactivity.
    • 
                            
  • 1918 – Co-discovered the element protactinium with Otto Hahn.
    • 
                            
  • 1926 – Became the first female professor of physics in Germany
    • 
                            
  • 1938 – Fled Nazi Germany due to Jewish heritage, relocating to Sweden.
    • 
                            
  • 1939 – Helped interpret Hahn’s experiment, leading to the discovery of nuclear fission.
    • 
                            
  • 1944 – Otto Hahn received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry, but Meitner was not recognized.
    • 
                            
  • 1946 – Named "Woman of the Year" by the American press.
    • 
                            
  • 1960 – Retired and moved to England
    • 
                            
  • 1968 – Passed away at age 89; her gravestone bears the inscription: "A physicist who never lost her humanity."
    • 
                            
  • 1997 – The element Meitnerium (Mt) was named in her honor.
